Thursday, May 30, 2013, 07:16

Press digest

The following are the top stories in the Maltese and overseas press.

The Times of Malta reports how the EU has insisted that Malta brings its deficit to acceptable level.

The Malta Independent says OLAF director-general Giovanni Kessler has invited critics to take any human rights breach claims to court.

In-Nazzjon says the government has to act within four months to bring down the deficit.  It also reports more worker transfers within the Social Policy Ministry.

l-orizzont says a Maltese diplomat who was seized for a day in Libya in November, was ignored by the Nationalist government and remains jobless.

The overseas press

The eurozone will remain in recession in 2013, with the estimated GDP falling 0.6 percent and unemployment at 12.1 percent. Euronews quotes the OECD's Economic Outlook report saying this marks an increase of almost a percentage point compared to the previous year. In 2014, the OECD added, the eurozone economy should register a growth of 1.1 percent, even though the unemployment rate is destined to continue rising, reaching 12.3 percent.

Le Soir reports the EU has eased its financial austerity demands giving France, Spain, the Netherlands, Poland, Portugal and Slovenia more time to bring their deficit levels under control so that they can support their ailing economies. The EU Commission said they must instead overhaul their labour markets and implement fundamental reforms to make their economies more competitive.

Meanwhile, The Daily Telegraph says President Hollande has warned the Commission not to “dictate” orders on how France should run its economy after the Brussels executive called for urgent eurozone reforms to avert a “social emergency”.

ABC reports controversial changes to electoral funding in Australia have been killed off by a coalition decision to renege on an agreement with the government. Under the proposal, parties and successful independent MPs would have been paid $1 (€0.74) for every primary vote they received. Legislation was due to be introduced to parliament today, but the Coalition asked for it be pulled in the face of growing backbench disquiet. And this morning Opposition Leader Tony Abbott declared that the Coalition has “listened, we've learned, and the Bill is now dead”.

Israel is preparing to build more than 1,000 new settler homes in annexed east Jerusalem as the United States strives to revive dormant Israeli-Palestinian peace talks. Danny Seidemann, director of Jerusalem settlement watchdog Terrestrial Jerusalem, told AFP that contracts for 300 homes have been signed and another 797 plots were to be offered for sale near the West bank city of Jerusalem. Both are in mainly-Arab areas annexed by the Israeli after the six-day war of 1967 – a move still unrecognised by the international community.

USA Today quotes police saying a threatening letter sent to New York Mayor Michael Bloomberg shows traces of the deadly toxin ricin. The substance was also detected on a letter sent to a lobby group which is against illegal guns and which Bloomberg supports.

The Syrian rebels’ military commander has accused the Lebanese Shite movement Hezbollah of invading Syria with Iranian backing. In a BBC interview, Gen Selim Idriss claimed that the rebels did not have sufficient weapons to withstand an attack by about 7,000 Hezbollah militants on the rebel-held town of Qusair. Gen Idriss appealed to Western powers for more weapons “to defend our citizens”.

Associated Press reports an American soldier accused of killing 16 Afghan civilians last year has agreed to pleed guilty to murder charges. Prosecutor say Segeant Robert Bales left his remote base in souther Afghanistan in the early hours of March 11, 2012, attacking two villages nearby. Most victims were women and children.

Al-Horria says three feminists from the Ukrainian group Femen were arrested on Wednesday in Tunis after baring their breasts outside the justice ministry in support of detained Tunisian feminist Amina Tyler, who had posted topless pictures of herself online last March. It was the first protest by Femen members in an Arab country. They up to six months in jail for committing “obscene acts”.

Corriere della Sera says prosecutors in Milan have asked prison terms of two years and six months for Italian fashion designers Domenico Dolce and Stefano Gabbana, who allegedly dodged around a billion euros of taxes. The pair are on trial with five other people including company board member Cristiana Ruella and the designers' accountant, Luciano Patelli, over the tax evasion claims which date back to 2004. Dolce and Gabbana allegedly used a Luxembourg-based holdling company to dodge taxes owed to the Italian state. They deny the charges.

Denik says a Czech woman has beaten staggering odds to become pregnant with quintuplets without using IVF. Alexandra Kinova, 23, is due to give birth by Caesarean section on Sunday to the Czech Republic's first ever set of quins. Ms Kinova, who already has one son, did not realise she was carrying five babies until last month. The odds of conceiving quins naturally is one is several million, according to experts.

Xinhua reports the mother of the Chinese new-born found trapped in a sewer pipe claimed she fell pregnant following a one night stand - and then the father of the baby boy abandoned her. The 22-year-old woman - who has not been named – also said she could not afford an abortion or cope with the stigma of being a single mother. Chinese media said she went to the shared bathroom in a residential complex when she felt abdominal cramps last Saturday and gave birth. But she said the baby slipped into the sewer pipe and she was unable to free him.

The US rock star Bon Jovi has told Spain's El Mundo his band will play a concert for free in Madrid next month as an act of solidarity with Spanish fans who are suffering economic hardship. Proceeds from the concert would go to the workers who help stage it. An average ticket cost for a concert in Manchester next week costs €76.
