Lawyer Ann Fenech goes for PN executive president

Maritime lawyer Ann Fenech. Photo: Matthew Mirabelli

Maritime lawyer Ann Fenech has submitted her nomination for the post of president of the Nationalist Party executive.

Dr Fenech, who was only elected for the first time to the party’s executive last Saturday, has never been involved in any of the party structures officially before and had been pushed to run for the post of leader to replace Lawrence Gonzi.

She confirmed when contacted yesterday that she had submitted her nomination but would not be drawn into saying what she was expected to change in the party.

“It would be rather presumptuous of me to say what I’m going to change or what I’d like to change. The party has to do that and it has to be a team effort. It first has to come to terms with what happened and map out a way forward,” she said.

Dr Fenech, managing partner of Fenech and Fenech Advocates and heads its marine litigation department, said it was a phase of her life in which she wanted to get involved in politics.

“It’s all a learning curve. I am sure there is a lot of good that must be retained. However, there are also issues which caused grief to people and I think the party needs to address this.”

There are also issues which caused grief to people and I think the party needs to address this

Dr Fenech is compiling, along with a number of other people, a report on the PN’s general election defeat.

“The last part of the report deals with the way forward. The party has to reach out, has to open its doors and needs to listen to all the people out there. Now people are expecting us to stop talking and get down to putting our words into action. We need a plan of action. I come from the private sector where I do plans of action on a daily basis, so this is where I believe I can give the party my contribution,” she said.

Contenders for other posts include former minister Chris Said for general secretary and incumbent Karol Aquilina for the post of president of the PN administrative committee. Outgoing deputy general secretary Jean Pierre Debono is expected to reapply for the post while several people are being mentioned for the two remaining posts.

Former leadership contender Raymond Bugeja is being mentioned as a possible contender for the treasurer post, along with Alex Perici Calascione while lawyer Trevor De Giorgio and former PN president Victor Scerri are likely contenders for the international secretary position.

The elections will be held on June 5 and 70 people are eligible to vote.

PN finance commission set up

PN leadership contender Raymond Bugeja will head the new commission set up by leader Simon Busuttil to delve into the party’s financial situation and recommend ways to solve it. Its members are MPs Tonio Fenech and Claudio Grech, former Air Malta chairman Lawrence Zammit, Chris Scicluna and Mark Portelli.

The party has failed to pay its employees on time for two successive months and has even considered selling some of its properties to bridge the gap.
