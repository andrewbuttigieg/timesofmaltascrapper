Government committed to ensure retirement age remains 65

Marie Louise Coleiro Preca

Government is committed to ensure that the retirement age remains 65 and this plan has been communicated to the European Commission.

The Commission recently recommended that Malta addresses the pensions issue, Social Solidarity Minister Marie-Louise Coleiro Preca said.

She said the Government was putting forward other suggestions such as maximising the women's work productivity. There was also the electoral pledge to introduce a third pillar pension and the Government was now studying options.

"If we are pressured I can assure you that funds will not be withdrawn from social sectors such as health and social services," she said.

She was speaking during the bi-annual general conference of the General Workers Union's pensioners association.

The minister said the government was committed to evaluate the current pension system to establish, among other things, if pensions were enough to allow a good quality of life and that they were sustainable. This would be done through consultation, she said. A working group had been set up for this purpose.

She said that 10 years had been wasted in addressing the pension problem and, had this been addressed before, "we would be in a position to ensure that your children and grandchildren have a secured future”.

"Meanwhile, we found the European Commission had issued a country specific recommendation to Malta to address pensions," she said.

GWU general secretary Tony Zarb said the union is calling for an amendment to the law regulating abuse on the elderly.
